Avalanche Rescue &
First Aid

Knowing how to respond to an avalanche can save lives. Learn rescue techniques, first aid essentials, and how to use transceivers, probes, and shovels effectively.

Essential #1

Probe

A lightweight, collapsible pole used to pinpoint a buried person's exact location beneath the snow. Probing is essential for fast and efficient rescue efforts.

Essential #2

Shovel

Vital for digging out avalanche victims and performing snowpack stability tests. A durable, metal-bladed shovel is recommended over plastic alternatives.

Essential #3

Avalanche Transceiver

A device that sends and receives signals, allowing rescuers to locate buried victims quickly. Every team member should carry one, know how to use it, and ensure it's properly checked before heading out.

Essential #4

Avalanche Airbag Pack

A backpack equipped with an inflatable airbag that helps keep you on the surface of an avalanche, reducing burial risk.

Essential Avalanche Safety Gear

When traveling in avalanche terrain, carrying the right gear can mean the difference between life and death. The avalanche transceiver (beacon) helps rescuers locate buried victims quickly by transmitting and receiving signals. A probe is used to pinpoint a buried person's exact location in the snow. A shovel is essential for digging out avalanche victims and assessing snowpack stability. For added protection, an avalanche airbag pack can help keep you on the surface of a slide, reducing burial risk. Always carry these essentials, practice using them, and travel with a trained group. Safety starts with preparation!
